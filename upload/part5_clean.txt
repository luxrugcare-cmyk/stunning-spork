The Hidden Health Risk in Your Mattress
Your mattress is one of the most overlooked items in your home when it comes to hygiene. While you regularly wash your sheets and pillowcases, the mattress beneath absorbs everything that passes through — sweat, body oils, skin cells, pet dander, pollen, and dust. Over time, this creates a warm, humid environment where dust mites thrive.
Dust mites are microscopic arachnids that feed on dead human skin. They’re invisible to the naked eye, but their presence can trigger a range of health issues including allergic rhinitis, asthma attacks, eczema flare-ups, morning congestion, and poor sleep quality. A single mattress can harbour millions of dust mites — and their waste products are one of the most common indoor allergens.
If you or a family member wakes up with a stuffy nose, itchy eyes, or unexplained coughing, your mattress could be the culprit.

What Is Master Guarding?
Master Guarding is a professional-grade fabric protection treatment that creates an invisible, odourless barrier on your curtains, upholstery, and soft furnishings. This barrier causes liquid spills to bead up on the fabric surface rather than soaking in — giving you precious time to blot the spill before it becomes a permanent stain.
The treatment is applied after cleaning by Kathy’s trained technicians using professional equipment that ensures even, thorough coverage. It penetrates individual fabric fibres without changing the look, feel, or texture of your furnishings. Once applied and cured, the protection lasts for a full 12 months under normal residential use.
Master Guarding is not a DIY product. It requires professional application to achieve the correct coverage depth and consistency that delivers genuine, lasting protection. The difference between a supermarket spray and our professional treatment is the difference between wishful thinking and proven performance.
What’s Included in Master Guarding Protection
Professional-grade stain repellent — An invisible protective barrier that forms a molecular shield around each fabric fibre. Spills sit on the surface rather than soaking in.
12-month effectiveness — Full year of protection under normal use. Unlike consumer-grade products that fail within weeks, Master Guarding is proven to perform for 12 months.
Prevents oil & water stains — Whether it’s red wine on the sofa, coffee on the curtains, or oily fingerprints on the armchair — both oil-based and water-based spills are repelled.
Reduces UV colour fade — The protective barrier also filters a portion of harmful UV rays, helping your curtains and upholstery retain their vibrant colour for longer.
Annual renewal available — Maintain continuous protection year after year with an annual reapplication service. Many Johannesburg clients build Master Guarding into their annual fabric care routine.
How Master Guarding Works
The science behind Master Guarding is straightforward but sophisticated. The treatment forms a fluoropolymer barrier around each individual fabric fibre — think of it as microscopic armour. This barrier is hydrophobic (water-repelling) and oleophobic (oil-repelling), meaning it actively resists both types of common household spills.
Importantly, the barrier is breathable. It doesn’t trap moisture or create a plastic-like coating. Your fabrics continue to feel natural and comfortable — they just repel stains far more effectively than untreated fabric.
When a spill occurs on treated fabric, the liquid forms a bead on the surface rather than spreading and soaking in. This gives you a window of time — typically several minutes — to simply blot the spill away with a clean cloth before any staining can occur.
Why Professional Application Matters
DIY fabric protection sprays from hardware stores sit on the surface of fabrics and wash away with the first clean or wear off within weeks. They rarely provide uniform coverage and often leave visible residue or an unpleasant chemical smell.
Our professional Master Guarding application ensures: - Complete, uniform coverage — Every fibre is treated, not just the surface - Correct penetration depth — The barrier forms at the molecular level - No residue or odour — Invisible and scentless once cured - Verified 12-month performance — Professional-grade chemistry, professionally applied - Safe for all fabric types — Kathy matches the formulation to your specific fabric
The Best Time to Apply Master Guarding
Master Guarding can be applied to any clean fabric, but the ideal time is immediately after a professional clean — when your curtains or upholstery are at their freshest. Fire Proofing — A Legal Requirement for Commercial Properties
South African law requires commercial properties to maintain appropriate fire safety standards, and soft furnishings are a significant fire hazard that must be addressed. Untreated curtains and upholstery can ignite quickly, accelerate fire spread, and release toxic smoke — endangering lives and property.
SANS-compliant fire proofing treats your curtains and soft furnishings with a certified fire-retardant solution that significantly reduces flammability. In the event of a fire, treated fabrics will resist ignition, slow the spread of flames, and produce less toxic smoke — buying precious time for evacuation and emergency response.
If your commercial property hasn’t been fire-proofed, or if your current certificate has expired, you may be operating in violation of building and fire safety regulations. This can invalidate your insurance coverage, result in fines, and — most critically — put lives at risk.
What’s Included in Our Fire Proofing Service
Fire compliance certificate — A formal, recognised certificate that meets insurance requirements and satisfies fire inspector audits. This document is your proof of compliance.

Annual renewal available — Fire-retardant treatments require periodic reapplication to maintain effectiveness. We offer annual renewal services to ensure your compliance certificate never lapses.

Why Persian & Oriental Rugs Need Specialist Care
A standard carpet cleaner sees your Persian rug as just another dirty floor covering. We see it very differently. These rugs are constructed using techniques and materials that demand specialist knowledge:
Natural Dyes: Traditional Persian and Oriental rugs use vegetable and mineral-based dyes that can bleed or shift colour if exposed to the wrong cleaning agents or excessive moisture. Kathy conducts dye-stability testing on every rug before any cleaning begins.
Hand-Knotted Construction: The pile is attached to a warp and weft foundation through thousands of individual knots. Aggressive cleaning methods, high-pressure extraction, or incorrect handling can loosen knots, cause pile distortion, or damage the structural foundation.
Wool & Silk Fibres: Premium wool has a natural lanolin content that gives it lustre and resilience. Silk fibres are extraordinarily fine and delicate. Both require pH-balanced, fibre-specific cleaning solutions that clean effectively without stripping natural oils or damaging delicate strands.
Age & Fragility: Antique rugs may have weakened foundations, brittle fibres, or areas of wear that require a gentler approach. Kathy identifies these vulnerabilities during her personal assessment and adjusts the treatment plan accordingly.

Our Persian & Oriental Rug Care Process
Step 1 — Kathy’s Personal Assessment: Every rug begins with Kathy’s expert evaluation. She identifies the rug type, fibre composition, dye type, construction method, and current condition — including any wear, damage, or previous repairs.
Step 2 — Dye Stability Testing: Before any moisture touches your rug, Kathy conducts dye-stability tests on inconspicuous areas to ensure colours won’t bleed or run during cleaning.
Step 3 — Dust & Dry Soil Removal: Rugs are thoroughly dusted to remove dry soil and grit that has worked its way into the pile. This step alone can remove surprising amounts of abrasive material.
Step 4 — Specialist Cleaning: Using the method selected during assessment — typically a controlled, low-moisture hand-cleaning process — the rug is gently cleaned with fibre-appropriate solutions.
Step 5 — Controlled Drying: Rugs are dried in a controlled environment with appropriate airflow and humidity to prevent fibre distortion, colour bleeding, or mildew formation.
Step 6 — Final Inspection: Kathy personally inspects the completed rug before it’s returned to you, ensuring it meets our exacting standards.
On-Site or Collection — You Choose
We offer both on-site and collection services for Persian and Oriental rug care, and Kathy will recommend the best option based on her assessment of your specific rug:
On-Site Cleaning: Suitable for robust, colour-fast rugs in good condition. We bring our specialist equipment to your Johannesburg home and clean the rug in place — convenient and effective for appropriate pieces.
Collection & Workshop Cleaning: Recommended for delicate, antique, silk, or heavily soiled rugs. Our controlled workshop environment allows for more precise temperature, humidity, and drying control — ensuring the safest possible treatment for your most precious pieces.
