2160: Jacaranda Pollen: Beautiful to Look At, Destructive to Fabric
2161: Between late September and early November, Pretoria lives up to its Jacaranda City nickname — and curtain owners live through their most challenging period of the year. Jacaranda pollen is fine, sticky, and surprisingly persistent. It doesn’t just sit on fabric surfaces; it works into fibres, attracted by static electricity, and creates a film that standard cleaning simply won’t shift. Worse, pollen is acidic. Left untreated, it can actually damage fabric fibres over time, causing weakening and discolouration that becomes permanent.
2162: Hatfield, with its dense Jacaranda planting around the university precinct, is particularly affected — student accommodation landlords often call us in November with curtains that have turned distinctly purple. Waterkloof and Menlyn, with their established gardens and tree-lined avenues, face similar challenges in their residential and hospitality properties. Our post-Jacaranda-season deep cleaning service is specifically formulated to extract pollen residues from fabric fibres, neutralise the acidity, and restore curtains to their pre-season condition.
2163: Diplomatic Residences and Government Buildings
2164: Waterkloof and the broader eastern Pretoria ridge host one of the most significant diplomatic communities in Africa — embassies, high commissions, and ambassadorial residences where curtain presentation is a matter of protocol. Kathy has worked in numerous diplomatic properties and understands the discretion, security awareness, and exacting standards that these environments demand. Our on-site process is particularly valuable in secure properties where removing curtains to external facilities simply isn’t permitted.
2165: Government buildings and official residences throughout Pretoria East and the CBD present similar requirements — formal drapery that must be maintained to a high standard, often involving heritage fabrics or installations of significant value. Kathy’s background as a fabric expert means she can assess and clean these installations with the care and knowledge they deserve.
2166: Centurion Corporate Parks and Midrand’s Growth Corridor
2167: Centurion has transformed from a quiet dormitory town into a major commercial hub, with office parks along John Vorster Drive, major corporate headquarters, and the expanding residential estates of Centurion East and West. 2168: Midrand, straddling the N1 between Johannesburg and Pretoria, is one of Gauteng’s fastest-growing areas.
