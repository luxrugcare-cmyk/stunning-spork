The Northern Suburbs Deserve Specialist Curtain Care
Johannesburg North is the city’s premium address — and the place where curtains work hardest. From the glass-walled penthouses of Sandton to the family estates of Bryanston, from the executive homes of Hyde Park to the modern townhouses of Sunninghill and Rivonia, this region has the highest concentration of full-length sheers, layered voiles, and imported designer drapes in Gauteng. Why Highveld Dust Is the Hidden Enemy
The elevation of Johannesburg North — from the ridges of Northcliff-adjacent Randburg to the hilltops of Fourways — means these suburbs catch the full force of Highveld wind and dust. Our on-site cleaning process extracts this dust at depth without removing curtains from their tracks — critical for delicate headings and interlined panels that can distort if handled incorrectly.

This dual character is exactly why On The Spot Curtain Cleaning Specialists invested in heavy-duty extraction equipment alongside our delicate-fabric systems. The O.R. Tambo Hotel Corridor Demands Excellence
Bedfordview and Edenvale are arguably Gauteng’s most competitive hospitality markets after Sandton. Established Suburbs. Beautiful Drapes. Specialist Care Required.
Johannesburg South is home to some of the city’s most established and beloved residential suburbs — Alberton, Meyersdal, Glenvista, Mulbarton, Bassonia, and Kibler Park. These aren’t new developments with off-the-shelf sheers. These are family homes that have been built and furnished over decades, with curtains to match: heavy velvet panels, textured chenille drapes, interlined blackout curtains, and elaborately swagged valances that were made to measure by proper curtain makers.
Those beautiful, weighty fabrics are also the most challenging to clean correctly. Take them to a conventional dry cleaner and they come back a size smaller, the pile flattened, the interlining shrunk and bunching. Kathy Dunlop has seen it hundreds of times — a devastated Meyersdal homeowner holding curtains that no longer reach the floor, or a Glenvista family discovering their blackout lining has separated from the face fabric after improper handling.
That’s exactly why On The Spot Curtain Cleaning Specialists cleans on-site. Heavy drapes with interlining, buckram headings, and complex pleating are never removed from their tracks. We clean them where they hang, supporting the full weight of the fabric throughout the process, ensuring the structural integrity that makes these curtains special is preserved completely.
The Fabrics of the South — Velvet, Chenille, Blackout, Brocade
Walk through the established avenues of Alberton or the hillside homes of Glenvista and you’ll find curtains that represent a significant investment in home furnishing. Thick velvet that frames a lounge window perfectly. Chenille that catches the afternoon light in a Mulbarton family room. Double-width blackout drapes in a Bassonia main bedroom that block every trace of street lighting. These fabrics demand more than a standard clean — they need a specialist who understands fibre behaviour, pile direction, and the interaction between face fabric, interlining, and backing.
Kathy personally assesses every job in the southern suburbs. With her background as an interior decorator and fabric expert, she can identify fibre content and construction methods at a glance — essential for determining the correct cleaning approach. A polyester velvet responds very differently to a cotton chenille, and a lined blackout panel requires completely different handling to an unlined sheer.
Retirement Villages and Family Homes Welcome
Johannesburg South has a significant retirement community, with complexes in Alberton, Glenvista, and surrounding areas where residents have often brought their original curtains from previous homes. For the area’s many family homes in Kibler Park, Mulbarton, and newer developments, we offer the same careful service — whether it’s removing sticky fingerprints from lounge drapes or freshening up curtains after a renovation project.

Mining Dust Legacy Meets Modern Corporate Growth
Johannesburg West carries a history that other regions simply don’t — over a century of mining activity that has left a lasting imprint on the land, the air, and yes, the curtains. Roodepoort, Florida, Northcliff, Weltevreden Park, and Constantia Kloof all sit within the broader mining belt, and while the headgear may have disappeared from many areas, the fine particulate legacy remains. This is silica-laden dust — finer, more abrasive, and more persistent than ordinary household dust. It works its way deep into curtain fibres and doesn’t come out with a standard vacuum or surface clean.
On The Spot Curtain Cleaning Specialists has developed specific protocols for mining-legacy dust. Our extraction system generates sufficient suction to pull these deeply embedded particulates from the pile and weave of your curtains without removing them from the window. Kathy Dunlop has been working in the western suburbs for over 15 years and can identify mining-related soiling versus standard household dust at a glance — the colour, distribution pattern, and texture all tell a story that informs our cleaning approach.
The Changing Face of the West — Corporate Parks and New Developments
While the mining legacy defines much of Roodepoort and Florida, Johannesburg West is also one of the city’s fastest-developing commercial corridors. The expanding business parks along Christiaan de Wet Road, the corporate developments near Weltevreden Park, and the professional offices in Constantia Kloof all require regular curtain maintenance that keeps reception areas, meeting rooms, and executive offices looking professional.
Northcliff, perched on its distinctive ridge, offers a different profile entirely — some of the city’s most stunning residential views come with curtains that catch the full afternoon sun and the western breeze. The Heritage Heartland of Johannesburg

Kathy Dunlop has a particular fondness for the Central suburbs. Heritage Homes: Victorian Drapes, Edwardian Pelmets, and Everything In-Between
The heritage homes of Parktown and the surrounding ridge suburbs represent some of Johannesburg’s finest residential architecture — and some of its most complex curtain cleaning challenges. The curtains in these homes have often been custom-made to fit irregular window shapes and may incorporate delicate trim, braiding, or hand-worked headings that simply can’t survive removal and industrial cleaning.
Our on-site process allows Kathy to work with the curtains exactly as they’re hung, preserving the relationship between fabric, lining, and fixture. Boutique Hotels, Guest Houses, and the Creative Suburbs

Jacaranda Pollen: Beautiful to Look At, Destructive to Fabric
Between late September and early November, Pretoria lives up to its Jacaranda City nickname — and curtain owners live through their most challenging period of the year. Jacaranda pollen is fine, sticky, and surprisingly persistent. It doesn’t just sit on fabric surfaces; it works into fibres, attracted by static electricity, and creates a film that standard cleaning simply won’t shift. Worse, pollen is acidic. Left untreated, it can actually damage fabric fibres over time, causing weakening and discolouration that becomes permanent.
Hatfield, with its dense Jacaranda planting around the university precinct, is particularly affected — student accommodation landlords often call us in November with curtains that have turned distinctly purple. Waterkloof and Menlyn, with their established gardens and tree-lined avenues, face similar challenges in their residential and hospitality properties. Our post-Jacaranda-season deep cleaning service is specifically formulated to extract pollen residues from fabric fibres, neutralise the acidity, and restore curtains to their pre-season condition.
Diplomatic Residences and Government Buildings
Waterkloof and the broader eastern Pretoria ridge host one of the most significant diplomatic communities in Africa — embassies, high commissions, and ambassadorial residences where curtain presentation is a matter of protocol. Kathy has worked in numerous diplomatic properties and understands the discretion, security awareness, and exacting standards that these environments demand. Our on-site process is particularly valuable in secure properties where removing curtains to external facilities simply isn’t permitted.
Government buildings and official residences throughout Pretoria East and the CBD present similar requirements — formal drapery that must be maintained to a high standard, often involving heritage fabrics or installations of significant value. Kathy’s background as a fabric expert means she can assess and clean these installations with the care and knowledge they deserve.
Centurion Corporate Parks and Midrand’s Growth Corridor
Centurion has transformed from a quiet dormitory town into a major commercial hub, with office parks along John Vorster Drive, major corporate headquarters, and the expanding residential estates of Centurion East and West. Glass-walled offices need sun-control curtains that are clean and functional — streaked, dusty fabric defeats the purpose entirely.
Midrand, straddling the N1 between Johannesburg and Pretoria, is one of Gauteng’s fastest-growing areas. From the corporate headquarters near the Allandale interchange to the residential estates of Kyalami and Vorna Valley, curtains here work hard — long commutes mean homes are closed up all day, trapping dust and cooking odours in fabric, while the altitude catches the full force of Highveld wind and dust.
